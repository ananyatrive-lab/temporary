The Startup Funding Lifecycle

Before you start investing, it is vital to understand that startups raise money in progressive stages as they grow. Each stage reflects a different level of company maturity and risk:

1. Pre-Seed (Idea Stage)

Founders build a basic prototype (MVP). High risk of failure. VCs look closely at team credentials, domain knowledge, and initial market research.

2. Seed (Validation Stage)

Product is launched, and early customer adoption begins. VCs evaluate customer feedback (NPS), early revenue growth, and market size validation (TAM).

3. Series A (Scaling Stage)

Startup has a proven business model and seeks to scale. Key focus is on customer economics efficiency (LTV/CAC ratio) and revenue growth velocity.

4. Series B (Expansion Stage)

Startup expands market share and accelerates operations. Focus shifts to operational capital efficiency, user retention, and competitor density.

About Groundwork

Groundwork is an educational technology portal designed to democratize venture capital analysis and startup screening. Founded with the mission to bridge the gap between complex machine learning diagnostics and aspiring angel investors, Groundwork provides risk-free simulator sandboxes and real-time regression evaluation tools.

By blending empirical data metrics (like customer LTV/CAC, cohort runway calculations, and founder experience) with predictive algorithms, the platform replaces gut-feeling valuations with objective, statistically validated analysis. Beginners can learn standard VC jargon, observe market growth paths, and practice making seed-to-series-B investments in real time.
